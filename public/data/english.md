## English / General
**Q:** Dodge
**A:**
- => uhnout, vyhýbat se, dodge ball = vybíjená (sport)
    - verb
- to avoid something unpleasant
    - *The Senator dodged questions about his relationship with the actress.*

## English / General
**Q:** Refrain
**A:**
- => from doing sth zdržet se čeho, vyhnout se čemu, upustit od čeho neučinit
    - verb
- to avoid doing or stop yourself from doing something
    - *We refrained from talking until we knew that it was safe.*
    - *The sign on the wall said "Please refrain from smoking."*

## English / General
**Q:** Jeopardize
**A:**
- => ohrozit
    - verb
- to cause something to be harmed or damaged, or to put something in danger
    - *She knew that failing her exams could jeopardize her whole future.*

## English / General
**Q:** Mitigate
**A:**
- => zmírnit, zklidnit, ulehčit
    - verb
- to make something less harmful, unpleasant or bad
    - *Steps were taken to mitigate the effects of the economic crisis.*
    - *Proper planning can mitigate potential risks.*

## English / General
**Q:** Accountable
**A:**
- => zodpovědný (to sb for sth) nebo hnát k odpovědnosti (hold sb accountable for sth)
    - adjective
- to be responsible for what you do with a satisfactory reason for it
    - *Politicians should be accountable to the public who elected them.*
    - *The recent tax reforms have made government more accountable for its spending.*
    - *You have to hold your employees accountable for their actions, or else they will just do whatever they please.*

**Q:** Anticipate

    - verb
- to imagine or expect that something will happen:
    - *We don't anticipate any trouble.*

## English / General
**Q:** Varying
**A:**
- => proměnlivý, měnící se, měnivý
    - adjective
- changing or different in amount or level, especially from one occasion to another, or from one person or thing to another
    - *The technology is able to convert handwriting into digital text with varying degrees of success.*
    - *They tackled the task with varying degrees of enthusiasm.*
    - *Hospitals' varying sizes makes it difficult to compare them in a meaningful way.*

## English / General
**Q:** Superficial
**A:**
- => povrchní
- => na venek jevící se
- adjective
- (of a person) never thinking about things that are serious or important
    - *He's fun to be with but he's very superficial.*
- only on the surface of something
    - *superficial damage*
    - *The driver only received superficial injuries/cuts/wounds.*

## English / General
**Q:** Vital
**A:**
- => to/for sth životně důležitý, nezbytný, nepostradatelný pro co
- => vitální, plný života, *vital signs* známky života - (základní) fyziologické funkce
    - adjective
- necessary for the success or continued existence of something; extremely important
    - *She had found out some information of vital importance.*
    - *The kidney plays a vital role/part in the removal of waste products from the blood.*
    - *It is absolutely vital that you do exactly as I say.*
    - *It is vital to get medical supplies to the area as soon as possible.*

## English / Phrasal verb
**Q:** Pick something up
**A:**
- => pochytit znalost
- to learn a new skill or language by practising it rather than being taught it
    - *Don't bother with the computer manual - you'll pick it up as you go along.*
    - *When you live in a country you soon pick up the language.*
- to learn interesting or useful information from someone or something
    - *The nurse had picked up the information from a conversation she overheard.*
